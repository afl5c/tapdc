What is TAP Edge?

In 2014, the Taiwanese American Professionals of Washington, D.C. (TAP-DC) launched its first ever mentor program, TAP Edge. Meant specifically to bridge the DMV Taiwanese/Taiwanese-American community, Edge pairs local undergraduate students with successful professionals for one-on-one career, academic, and life guidance.

Bike THERE


Jason Kuo
President

What is Bike THERE?

Bike THERE is an annual event for Taiwanese Americans to explore their heritage and strengthen their Taiwanese-American identities and communities. Bike THERE, Be THERE.

The Taiwanese Heritage Expedition (THERE) is a program put together by Taiwanese American Professionals of Washington, D.C. (TAP-DC) to promote and sustain economic and cultural ties between Taiwanese and Taiwanese-Americans of all generations, past, present, and future. Bike THERE is a culturally unique and decisively significant opportunity that is and will continue to be a testament to the strength of Taiwanese and Taiwanese-American relations. Bike THERE consists of a 9-day bicycle trip around Taiwan and is a once-in-a-lifetime experience for accepted participants to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of Taiwan, its people, and its culture.

Professional Development


Jennifer Yi
Professional Chair

What is the professional development program?

The TAP-DC professional development committee hosts a number of events throughout the year focused on connecting and developing young professionals in the Washington, D.C. metro area. In addition, the professional development committee hosts a professional development day for undergraduates in the DC area to help prepare for their transition from college student to young professional. Our professional development committee also works with other professional groups in the area such as NAAAP and CAPAL.
